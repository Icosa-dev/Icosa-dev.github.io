Log 1 - Premature Optimization is the Devil

LJC || 11.28.24

Premature optimization is the root of all evil in programming. Donald Knuth himself said this in _The Art of Computer Programming_ which was originally published in 1968. Yet somehow people still fall for it.

Problems should create solutions. Solutions shouldnt exist independent of problems because more often than not that solution becomes a problem. This is why premature optimization is bad. If you dont have optimization problems and you burden yourself with optimizing when its not needed its giving yourself obsticals you dont need to have there in the first place. If its not broken then dont burden yourself with fixing it.

Premature optimization is why people don't recommend python. Python isnt a bad language by any means. Python has many problems with it like dynamic typing. But people always point at one thing as why they think python is bad. Its speed.

Python is slow. Python is much much slower than its contemporaries. But the problem is that python isnt ment to be fast. Python is supposed to be a simple readable high level language mainly for beginners. Python's speed isnt the main problem facing new programmers. The main problem they face is finding out what the f*ck they are doing. And having someone tell them "Oh dont use python its too slow start with C" isnt helping them, its hindering them.

Pythons speed most of the time isnt a problem. Using just about any major programming language guarentees speed and automation that cant be matched by a human. So dont tell new programmers to not use python because of speed. Because speed shouldnt be a problem until its a problem.
